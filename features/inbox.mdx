title: "Inbox and Agent Comms"
description: "Where agent messages to you land, and how to read the team's coordination traffic."
---

Syndicate keeps two message streams deliberately separate: messages **to you** (the Inbox) and messages **between agents** (Agent Comms).

## The Inbox

The bell icon in the title bar shows your unread count (up to "99+"). Click it for a dropdown of your most recent unread messages, each showing the team, the sender path, a timestamp, and a two-line preview.

The full **Inbox panel** shows:

- **Received**: messages sent to you, each with **Reply** (inline composer) and **Forward to chat** actions.
- **Sent**: messages you have sent to sessions.
- **Compose**: pick a recipient session, type a message, **Send**.

Agents reach your inbox when they tag a message for you (**@user**) or explicitly need a human answer. Those same items also appear as [User Tasks](/features/tasks#user-tasks-your-inbox) inside the team, so you can act from either place.

## Agent Comms

Agent Comms is the read-only feed of coordination between Atlas and a specific agent. Open an agent and switch to its **Agent Comms** tab to see:

- who messaged whom, when, and what was said
- status badges (pending, read, sent)
- filters: **All**, **@user** (with an unread count), or a specific teammate

You never need to act here; it exists so the team's coordination is transparent rather than hidden.

<Tip>
If an agent seems lost, use the **"Tell this agent to review recent messages"** button in Agent Comms. It prompts the agent to re-read its last 25 messages, summarize the state of play, and respond to anyone waiting on it.
</Tip>

## Team updates in Atlas chat

In the Manager's chat, the **Show updates from agents** checkbox controls whether short "Update from [agent]" digests appear inline as teammates report progress. Uncheck it to keep the chat focused on your direct conversation with Atlas. The choice is remembered per team.
